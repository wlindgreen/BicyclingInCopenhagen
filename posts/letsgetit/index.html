Introduction

As you might have heard, Copenhagen has in recent years been ranked the most bicycle-friendly city in the world. Latest in 2021 where Copenhagen was placed ahead of other European cities like Tallinn, Amsterdam and Helsinki according to the Cycle Cities Report compiled by money.co.uk. Copenhagen has fostered a bicycle culture unlike many other countries in the world, where people look strangely at you if you donâ€™t own a bike. Larger bicycle path extensions, cycle superhighways, innovative solutions, bike bridges and much more have contributed to Copenhagen being the top city to ride a bike. In addition to the health benefits of choosing the bike over the car, the bike is also a much more sustainable means of transport. Improved conditions for cyclists, which create greater demand for cycling, will therefore also contribute to a greener world.

The site aims to give you a broader perspective and view of how Copenhageners use the bicycle paths. Moreover, the site presents machine learning models, which can give an indication of factors that influences the number of cyclists on a given day. Hopefully, you will get answers to the following questions:

  • How do you avoid the rush hour on the bike paths?
  • In what season and months are cycling most popular?
  • What effect do the so-called cycle superhighways have on the bicycle demand on a route?
  • Does the weather affect Copenhageners’ cycling habits, or maybe the petrol price?
  • How much pressure on the bike paths can we expect in the future?

As Copenhageners we might have a perception of the bicycling behaviour, but does it aligns with reality. If we the above have caught you attention, you are encourage to get a cop of coffee, soda or beer and continue reading.
